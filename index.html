Hi, I'm Anisa!

STUDENT | SMALL BUSINESS OWNER

I'm Anisa, a designer and student born in Vancouver and currently studying in Waterloo. Outside of school, I run a small business called Cakes by Anisa, where I bake, design, market, and sell custom treats.

 

Balancing university and entrepreneurship has taught me how to move quickly from idea to execution. Whether I'm sketching out a new product, refining a visual identity, or crafting an experience that feels thoughtful and personal. At the core of everything I do is a love for building things that make people feel seen, celebrated, and a little bit delighted.

Works

Place Portrait

For this place portrait, I focused on Lazaridis Hall in Waterloo, studying its sharp geometry, glass façade, and how students move through the space. I used simplified forms and a warm, minimal palette to show Lazaridis not just as a business school, but as a social hub. The illustration highlights the bright atrium, layered levels, and everyday circulation patterns, turning an academic building into a snapshot of energetic campus life.

Website Re-design

I redesigned Silver Valet Dental Care's website to feel warmer and more trustworthy while making it easier for patients to find what they need. I simplified the navigation, clarified services and CDCP information, and added clear calls-to-action. The new layout uses a soft, modern colour palette, improved typography, and consistent components to create a calm, professional experience across desktop and mobile.

Campus Community App

For this case competition, my team and I designed a community app that helps Ismaili Muslim students stay connected no matter which college or university they attend. The platform organizes students into campus- and city-based spaces, with event calendars, mentorship matching, and support channels for newcomers and underrepresented groups.

Volunteering

ALC learning center teacher

I volunteered as an English teacher at an Advanced Learning Center, working with Grade 5 students to build confidence and do better in school. I planned lessons on reading, writing, and vocabulary, and used games and small-group activities to keep them engaged. Through regular practice and feedback, I focused on helping each student strengthen their skills so they could participate more comfortably in class and feel proud of their progress over time.

Encounters TedEd Club Administrator

I was an administrator for the Encounters TED-Ed Club in Vancouver, where I helped organize meetings, plan workshops, and guide students through the process of turning their ideas into TedTalks. While supporting others, I also developed and delivered my own talk on ethnocentrism, exploring how our cultural lens shapes the way we see the world.

3PointRevelation Website

Beyond school, I volunteered as part of the Three Point Revelation project, an online student initiative focused on historical injustices, current issues, and future solutions. I started as a writer and later became Senior Writing Manager, leading a small team to plan articles, edit drafts, and keep our website content consistent and thoughtful. The role helped me grow as a collaborator and communicator while connecting with students from around the world.
